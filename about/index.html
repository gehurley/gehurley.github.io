about

Hello. I am an archivist and librarian. I currently work as the Digital Preservation Librarian for Scholars Portal, a service of the Ontario Council of University Libraries. OCUL is a 21-member consortium of academic libraries in Ontario. In this role, I am responsible for supporting the preservation of shared licenced content content such as ejournals and books and helping our members preserve their own digital assets. I oversee developing and maintaing services and projects, research issues and questions, keep on top of digital preservation happenings, design and implement workflows, and consult on licencing issues. I also serve as Director without Portfolio and Off the Record Editor with the Archives Association of Ontario, as an Ex Officio Director and Book Design Awards Catalogue Project Manager for The Alcuin Society, and as a researcher with the InterPARES Trust project.

I started my career as a summer archives assistant at the Charlotte County Archives in my hometown of St. Andrews, New Brunswick. I was 16 and it was my first real summer job - and it turns out, led to my career! I returned to the Archives for three summers after that, and in that time, fell in love with the exciting and dynamic work that happens in archives on a daily basis. After pursuing a research interest in Canadian literature through a BA and MA, I completed the Dual Master of Archival Studies and Master of Library and Information Studies program at the University of British Columbia in 2015.

Outside of my work, I love to cook up big meals, shake up cocktails, collect Canadian music of the 1960s and 70s, go on hikes, and read thick books. I’ve been slowly working through WSET certification levels in wine knowledge, so hit me with your wine questions in addition to your archival ones.
